Synopsis

The Concept

A narrative-driven music video for the explosive Baltimore-based hardcore band END IT — blending comedy, action, skateboarding, and hard riffs into a story that's never been done in the hardcore genre. From Twitter Fingers to Real-World Consequences.

01
Act One

The Claim

"I know that guy."

Two friends — John and Mark — are watching recap videos of a famous skateboarder. Mark claims to be friends with the athlete and attempts to call him — only to get sent straight to voicemail.

Instead of taking the L, Mark spirals — building conspiracies that the skateboarder can't actually land his tricks. He takes to Twitter, slandering the skater's reputation while John eggs him on.

But there are repercussions to running your mouth online.

02
Act Two

The Hunt

Word gets back.

The skater sees the tweets. He doesn't respond online — he picks up the phone and calls END IT in a mob-boss style fashion to "resolve the issue."

Cut to John and Mark walking through the neighborhood — until a black van barrels through the streets hunting them down. The chase is frantic, cinematic, and inevitable.

They're captured.

03
Act Three

The Reckoning

Prove it or shut up.

Mark wakes up blindfolded, tied to a chair inside the skater's private facility.

The skater lands trick after trick while END IT plays out the song in various spots throughout the space — raw, aggressive, in-your-face.

But the final trick? He doesn't land it. The conspiracy was true all along. The singer from END IT ends the scene with a POV knockout punch straight to camera.
